Complete MSU-SND Student Affairs

Admissions Office

Director: Office of Student Affairs (OSA)

MSU-SND Admission Policy: No discrimination based on age, sex, nationality, religion, or political affiliation. Special accommodation for marginalized students.

Requirements for Freshmen:

  • Passing SASE/CET score (not below system median)
  • Senior High School Report Card (Form 138-A, Original)
  • Certificate of Good Moral Character from SHS Principal
  • PSA-authenticated Birth Certificate
  • Medical Certificate from University Clinic
  • 4 copies of 2x2 ID Pictures with name tag
  • Marriage Certificate (if applicable)

Scholarships & Financial Aid

Available Scholarships:

  • SASE Scholarship: For top SASE exam scorers
  • Academic Excellence: President's List (1.00 GPA), Chancellor's List (1.25+ GPA), Dean's List (1.75+ GPA)
  • Athletic Scholarships: For varsity players
  • Special Skills Grants: For cultural performers (Sakasakaw Ensemble, Orly's Band)
  • TES Grants: Government scholarships
Note: Students may only avail ONE government scholarship at a time. Scholarships are non-transferable between campuses.

Registration & Academics

Office of the Registrar handles all academic records and registration.

Academic Load:

  • Normal load: 18 units (non-lab) or 20 units (with lab)
  • With 1.5+ GPA: 21 units (non-lab) or 23 units (with lab)
  • Graduating students: Maximum 28 units
  • Summer term: 6 units normal, 9 units maximum

Student Classification:

  • Freshman: ≤25% of total units
  • Sophomore: 26-50% of total units
  • Junior: 51-75% of total units
  • Senior: ≥76% of total units

Student Discipline

Grounds for Disciplinary Action:

  • Cheating or academic dishonesty
  • Carrying weapons on campus
  • Alcohol/drug use or possession
  • Threats, assault, or violence
  • Immoral or dishonest conduct
  • Violation of university policies

Disciplinary Committees:

  • College Investigating Committee
  • Inter-college Disciplinary Committee

Sanctions: Warning, Reprimand, Suspension, Expulsion
